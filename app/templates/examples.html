Geometric Algebra and 3D WebGL Graphics

Creating and Animating a Scene with Eight.Js

The Eight.Js library that ships with MathDoodle has a coherent layered architecture that allows you to choose the appropriate level for flexibility and reuse. Eight.Js integrates Geometric Algebra with WebGL Graphics providing an efficient starting point for explorations into Geometry, Physics, Mathematics and Computing.

This example demonstrates many features of the Eight.Js library. It builds a scene from high level components and drives the animation using Geometric Algebra for computation.

May be a good starting point for Physics explorations.

This example is rather verbose and contains boilerplate code that could be eliminated with an appropriate application framework. However, the explicit nature of the example makes it easier to understand the various components and how they interact.

Physics Exploration

Projectile Motion

Physics demonstrations and explorations require accessible solutions without having to re-invent graphical components. This example shows how to model projectile motion by composing high-level components in the Eight.Js library.

Binary Star

A classic two-body simulation demonstrating gravitation, the center of mass, and reduced-mass concepts.

Earth-Moon

Demonstrates using two viewports to display a scene from different perspectives. Simulates the Earth-Moon gravitation system with directional lighting provided by the sun. The scene is rendered in plan view and and from the Earth.

Units of Measure

Replacing the labels used for basis vectors can be an instructive way to learn the relationships between geometric quantities, magnitudes, directions, aspect, and orientation.

Basis Labeling

Replacing the labels used for basis vectors can be an instructive way to learn the relationships between geometric measures, geometric quantities, magnitudes, directions, aspect, orientation, and units of measure.

Basis labels can also be customized appropriate to the context, e.g. compass directions. In this example we use Unicode graphical symbols to represent vectors, bivectors and the pseudoscalar and show how they interact under the geometric product.

Projectile Motion with Units

Brings units of measure into simulations. While this may not be desirable for high-performance applications, it does motivate the use of consistent units in order to allow the simulation to run!

Teaching 3D Computer Graphics

Teaching a Computer Graphics Course with WebGL

This example was inspired by the article by Edward Angel and Dave Shreiner in the book WebGL Insights by Patrick Cozzi.

The article describes the benefits of moving from OpenGL to WebGL. MathDoodle enhances the experience for the student by providing a powerful development environment, Geometric Algebra libraries, and a Graphics Library with entry points at many levels of flexibility and reuse.

Ray Tracing: The Science behind Computer Animation

While WebGL provides a high performance real-time graphics environment, movie-quality animations require Physics calculations based upon Geometric Optics and are often computed off-line taking many hours.

This example shows how simple ray tracing can be performed in the browser with the results written out to the HTML5 Canvas in a few seconds. The example is due to Microsoft. It would be interesting to adapt it to Geometric Algebra and enable learning Geometric Optics.

Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

Colors a complex (G2+) function in the Wessel (Argand) plane in order to visualize the direction. This may be used as a basis for introductory arguments for the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.

This example takes advantage of the GPU using custom shader programs for fast rendering.

Julia Set

A rendering of the Julia Set in 2D. Interestingly, this may be generalized to 3D and higher dimensions through the use of Geometric Algebra.
